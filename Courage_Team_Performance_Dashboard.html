Grading System
G
How Grades Are Calculated

Each metric uses two separate grading functions that answer different questions, and both are fixed once established — a grade is computed only from matches played up to and including the graded match, so later results never rewrite it. Match Grade is a z-score: the match value versus the mean and standard deviation of the team's performances to that point (z ≥ +1.25 Excellent · +0.5 Good · ±0.5 Typical · −1.25 Poor · below Abysmal, direction-aware). 3-Match Form applies the same z-bands to the rolling average of the last three consecutive matches, against the same to-date baseline — because rolling averages are smoother, the identical bands are effectively a stricter test of sustained form. Both grades require four matches of history; earlier matches show as pending. A match can still grade Poor on Match Grade yet Good on 3-Match Form — a single-game dip inside a form window that tracks above baseline.

A Z-score approach (standard deviation from the mean) was considered but rejected at this stage — with only 7 matches in the dataset, the standard deviation is still sensitive to single-game outliers. Min-max will remain the method until at least 8–10 matches are logged.

Formula: Position = (Match Value − Season Worst) ÷ (Season Best − Season Worst)

Important Limitations

Grades are relative, not absolute. A grade of Excellent means this was NCC's best performance of the season for that metric — it does not mean it is objectively excellent by league standards. Equally, Abysmal means it was the team's worst showing this season, not that it was catastrophic in isolation.

Small sample size. With 7 matches, the season range is building but grades will continue to recalibrate as more data arrives. A metric graded Excellent now may shift as stronger or weaker performances are recorded over the full season.

Pressing metrics are possession-adjusted (PA). Pressures, Counterpressures, Aggressive Actions, Tackles+Interceptions, Pressures in Opp. Half, and Counterpressures in Opp. Half are scaled using the formula: raw ÷ opponent possession% × 0.5. This normalises to a 50/50 possession baseline so a team with 40% possession is directly comparable to one with 60%. Possession percentages are sourced from StatsBomb match report PDFs.

3-Match Form activates from GW3 onwards. The Form column shows the average of the last 3 non-null values, graded against the season mean rather than the min/max range. The deviation bands are wider (±10% = Typical, ±35% = Excellent/Abysmal) to prevent hypersensitivity — rolling averages naturally smooth toward the centre, so tighter bands would produce artificially compressed grades. A Good or Excellent Form grade alongside a lower Match Grade signals positive trajectory: recent games are running above the season baseline even if the individual match underperformed.

Performance Areas
S
SOP Grading

The Standard of Play (SOP) grading system tracks NCC's ability to progress the ball through defined pitch zones and convert that progression into finishing opportunities. The pitch is divided into four zones: Zone 2 (defensive-to-midfield progression), Zone 3 (midfield-to-final third), Pass Zone (pre-box delivery area), and Shoot Zone (shooting positions).

Each zone tracks Attempts (volume), Completions (success), Completion % (efficiency), and Points (a composite score). A high volume of Zone 3 Attempts with a low Completion % signals the team is trying to enter the final third but being blocked — a pattern worth noting for tactical review.

Source: Match Stats spreadsheet — Z2, Z3, PZ, SZ columns. Note: Completion % columns in the spreadsheet show 0 — calculated as Completions ÷ Attempts.

P
Packing & Progression

Packing metrics quantify how many opposition players NCC removes from the game through passing and carrying. Bypassed Opponents captures all players beaten by the ball; Bypassed Defenders focuses on the defensive unit specifically, making it a stronger indicator of genuine progression quality.

Ball Win metrics (Removed Opponents/Defenders) measure the same concept in the defensive-to-offensive transition — opponents removed by winning the ball back. Critical Ball Loss and Ball Loss Removed Teammates are negative contribution metrics; lower values are better.

The Line Breaking table focuses specifically on passes that beat at least one opposition line into the final third. LB OBV (On-Ball Value) contextualises volume with quality — a high OBV from fewer passes is often more dangerous than a high volume with low OBV.

Source: Match Stats spreadsheet. Note: config originally specified Impect PDFs — data is available in the spreadsheet and used directly.

B
Box Domination

Box Domination measures NCC's ability to penetrate the opposition box and create high-quality chances. The Penetration table tracks entries (touches, passes into and inside the box, crosses), while the Finishing table assesses the quality of what NCC did once inside.

Post-Shot xG (Non Penalty PSxG Faced) measures the quality of shot placement — going beyond pre-shot position to account for where in the goal the shot was aimed. Higher PSxG indicates NCC placed shots in more dangerous areas of the frame.

The Opposition GK metrics (GSAA and NP Save %) measure how well the opposing goalkeeper performed. A high GSAA means the opp GK saved more than statistically expected — this is negative for NCC and graded accordingly (lower is better from NCC's perspective).

Source: Match Stats spreadsheet for most metrics. Post-Shot xG (PSxG), GSAA, and NP Save % are sourced from StatsBomb match report PDFs (GK shot stopping table). Tackles Won from PDF match stats page (spreadsheet Tackles column = 0 for all matches).

R
Box Resilience

Box Resilience is the defensive mirror of Box Domination — tracking how well NCC prevented the opposition from penetrating their box and creating chances. All opponent-side metrics in this section are graded inversely: lower values are better for NCC, reflecting less opposition threat allowed.

The Denying table tracks how often and successfully the opposition entered NCC's box. The Nullifying table assesses the quality of chances the opposition created and how well NCC's goalkeeper managed them.

NCC GK GSAA (Goals Saved Above Average) is the most important goalkeeper metric on the dashboard — positive means Sheridan saved more than the post-shot model expected, negative means she conceded more. GW3 (−0.66) reflects a night where she faced exceptionally well-placed shots and conceded 3 goals against a PSxG of 2.34. GW2 (0.00) is notable — Sheridan faced no shots on target, making NP Save % unavailable for that match.

Blocks per Shot and Clearances are positive metrics — higher values indicate NCC was more active in blocking and clearing before shots reached Sheridan. Very high Clearances can indicate sustained defensive pressure rather than dominance.

D
Defensive Intensity

Defensive Intensity covers how actively and how high up the pitch NCC defends. It is divided into three areas: Volume & Aggression (how much NCC pressed), Forward Leaning / Pressure Height (where they pressed), and Progression Allowed (what the opposition managed to do through the press).

Aggression Rate is the most possession-neutral metric in this section — measuring the proportion of opposition ball receipts that were pressed within 2 seconds, regardless of how many total receipts occurred. It is the best single indicator of NCC's defensive urgency.

Defensive Action Distance is a key pressure height indicator — the average distance from NCC's own goal where defensive actions occur. Higher = NCC is defending further up the pitch. GW3 (61.9) was the season high, coinciding with the most progressive pressing profile.

Opp. Pressured Passes in Def. Third measures how often NCC forced the opponent into pressured passes in NCC's defensive third — higher is better, indicating NCC's press stifled opposition progression. The Progression Allowed table tracks whether the opposition ultimately broke NCC's lines.

+/−
Positive vs Negative Metrics

Every metric in this dashboard has a defined polarity. Positive metrics are ones where higher values indicate better performance — more touches in the box, more line-breaking passes, higher defensive OBV. Negative metrics are ones where lower values indicate better performance — fewer opposition touches in the box, lower opposition xG, lower critical ball losses.

Opponent-side metrics (tagged Opp in the table) are always negative — they measure what the opposition achieved, so lower is better from NCC's perspective. The grading system automatically accounts for polarity; the season's worst defensive performance (highest opposition xG allowed) will always grade Abysmal regardless of whether the raw number is high or low.

Metrics tagged Neg (in red) are NCC's own negative contribution metrics — things like Critical Ball Loss and Ball Loss Removed Teammates. These are things NCC did that harmed their own performance, so lower values are graded as better.
